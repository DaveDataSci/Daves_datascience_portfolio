 COVID-19 Death Factors Analysis (England)

📌 Project Overview

This project investigates the key socio-economic and demographic factors influencing COVID-19 mortality across English local authorities.

Using real-world data, the analysis explores how variables such as age, housing conditions, health status, living arrangements, and transport methods contributed to variations in death rates during the pandemic.


---

🎯 Objectives

Identify the factors most strongly associated with COVID-19 deaths

Analyse how lifestyle, environment, and demographics influence mortality

Provide data-driven insights for public health policy



---

🗂️ Dataset

Source: NOMIS (UK official statistics database)

Data includes:

Age distributions

Housing and dwelling types

Health conditions

Living arrangements

Transport methods

Communal establishments




---

---

🔍 Key Findings

📊 Positive Correlation with Death Rates

Public transport usage

Shared or overcrowded housing

Living alone

Poor health conditions

Certain communal establishments (e.g. care homes without nursing)


📉 Negative Correlation with Death Rates

Private vehicle usage

Living as a couple

Good housing conditions


👥 Age Insights

Higher mortality observed among younger adults (15–25) due to lifestyle exposure

Reduced mortality in middle-aged groups (35–50)

Lower mortality in older groups due to shielding and healthcare access



---

💡 Key Insights

Social exposure and environment play a major role in disease transmission

Support systems (e.g. couples) improve survival outcomes

Infrastructure (housing, transport) significantly impacts public health



---

📌 Recommendations

Improve housing and living conditions

Reduce overcrowding and shared living risks

Encourage remote work where possible

Promote public health awareness and regular check-ups

Strengthen care systems in communal establishments
